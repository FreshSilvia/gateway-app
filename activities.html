What Awaits You?

Ebisu Circuit

The Ebisu Circuit is located in Sawamatsukura, Nihonmatsu-city, Japan. It cost $85 or 9000 Yen to enter and drive on 5 of the 7 courses all day. Admission fee for spectators is $15 or 1500 yen. Passengers fee is $19 or 2000 Yen, which includes admission fee and insurance fee. The target age group for drifting at the Ebisu Circuit is 18 and up. There are two major events held at the Ebisu Circuit, One is D1 Grand Prix and the second one is Formula Drift. The Ebisu Circuit has a total of 7 individiual tracks. You are also able to buy cars and keep them there, thanks to Power Vehicles.

Daikoku Futo Parking Area

The Daikoku Parking Area is located at a rest stop along one of Tokyo's central tolled highways, the Bayshore Route of the Shuto Expressway, commonly known as the Wangan. The Daikoku Parking Area cost can come up to 8,800 Yen or $83 to get to. There is no target age group. As long as you like cars, can stay out late, and provide yourself with transportation, you are good to go. Daikoku. The Daikoku Parking Area is 45 minutes away from Central Tokyo, position on a artifical island of Daikoku Futo. Many car enthusiast meet here to just hang out and enjoy talking and being around other people with the same interest.

Akihabara

It does not cost anything to get in to Akihabara. The only thing that cost in Akihabara is if you want to buy any items. There is not an actual age group for Akihabara. If your a big fan of Anime,Manga,videogames, and electornics you will enjoy your time here no matter the age. Akihabara is a district in central Tokyo that is famous for its many electronic shops. Akihabara is also called Akiba after a former local shrine. On Sundays, Chuo Dori, the main street through the district is closed to car traffic from 1pm to 6pm.

TeamLab Borderless

The Adult cost for tickets are 3,200 Yen or $30, which is 15 and over. For children 4-14 the cost is 1,000 yen or $9.37. The Cost for a Disabled Person is 1,600 Yen or $15. The Disabled Person must have a disability certificate. The target age group is anyone 4 and over. Teamlab Borderless is the worlds first Digital Art Museum. It is permanent digital art exhibition located in the Mori Building in Odaiba, Tokyo. It uses more than 500 computers and 470 projectors in specially designed areas. This is a place to wander and get lost into the digital artwork world.

Tokyo Skytree

The Tokyo Skytree is the world's tallest freestanding tower at 634 meters. It cost 4,200 Yen or $40 to get a combo ticket for anyone 12 and older. For 6-11 years of age the combo ticket price is 2,100 Yen or $20. The Tokyo Skytree is a broadcasting and telecommunications tower. There are two publicly accessible observation areas, the Tempo Deck and the Tempo Gallery. The Tempo deck has three levels, the observation floor, and two floors of restaurants. Theres is also an Skytree shop which offers a wide selection of mementos to take with you.

Japan Onsens

Depending on which Onsen you go to the cost could vary. If you go to an onsen inn then it could be free after you check in. There is no target age group for getting in a onsen. Most onsen's are divided by gender, but kids under the age of 7 are able to accompany a parent or guardian of either gender. Onsen is the Japanese name for hot spring. Since Japan is a volcanically active country the onsens use naturally hot water from geothermally heated springs. Traditional onsens are mostly located outside. They offer great scenery while you soak in the bath. Great for relaxing after a long day.
